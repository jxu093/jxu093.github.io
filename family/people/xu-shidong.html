Life Story

Xu Shidong was born in Dalian to a laborer father who died while he was still a child. The loss of his father as the sole breadwinner meant the family could no longer survive in the city. They relocated to Jilin to live with relatives, renting land cheaply to grow their own food for survival.

During the Japanese occupation of the 1940s, the family endured extreme hardship in Jilin. Despite the occupation, they had Japanese neighbors who were kind and provided food, helping them avoid starvation.

Communist Party Service

Growing up in Jilin, Xu Shidong joined the Communist Party and served as a young officer — carrying a firearm and fighting against the KMT (Kuomintang) and mountain raiders. This was distinct from formal army service.

During one engagement, he spent three days lying in frozen trenches. His leg was permanently injured as a result of the cold — a lasting physical mark of his service.

Education

Because he lacked early schooling, the Party rewarded his service by sending him to condensed middle and high school programs at age 25–26. The pace was intense and he was unable to finish. The family believes that had he completed the program, he could have achieved a very high government rank.

Career & Family in Yingkou

The government assigned him to Yingkou, where he worked various roles including as a government-run union leader at a factory. It was here he met and married Wang Guilan, introduced by her uncle who thought he was "very handsome."

They had three children together: two daughters and Xu Qiang (James).
